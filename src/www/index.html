What and Why:

Although UQ makes an iCalendar / vCalendar file available of our timetables through SiNet, it's a classic example of an IT project delivering what was asked, rather than what is needed. It cannot be subscribed to as you would subscribe to a normal calendar. Given that some faculties seem prone to giving timetable changes at rather short notice, it would be useful to have the calendar auto-sync to our phones and laptops. This is what this page does.

This is probably not even beta quality software. Your mileage may vary. Don't blame me if you miss an exam. You get what you pay for.

How to subscribe using Desktop Software:

  1. In iCal on your Mac (not tested elsewhere yet), click on the "Calendar" menu, then subscribe.
  2. Copy and paste the following URL into the box:
    https://uqcalendar.fearthecow.net/calendar/
  3. Enter your UQ username and password.
  4. Make sure to set the calendar to auto-update - e.g. hourly.
  5. Hit OK and enjoy your auto-syncing calendar!

Note: In order not to irritate the UQ IT staff, this site will fetch a new calendar from SiNet no more than once every 3 hours. It will cache a uniquely salted (and thus irreversible) hash of your password to authenticate you to provide the calendar again in the meantime.

Security notes:

This service requires your UQ username and password as it simulates a web-browsing session to the SiNet timetable page to get your timetable data. Ultimately, you have to trust that I am honest, handle your password correctly, and that I won't keep a copy of it, pretend to be you, and change your degree in sinet to be agricultural science.

Note, however, that I use this site myself, so I do have vested interest in maintaining good security (plus, I also used to work as a sysadmin).

Technical guarantees:

  • Encryption is pretty much same technical standard as your bank.
  • I will never store your password in any format that is reversible. I use a salted hash function with a random salt using the SHA-1 algorithm.
